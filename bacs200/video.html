Introduction
Hi, I’m Emma Ogle. I am currently studying Software Engineering at the University of Northern Colorado. I am currently a tutor for the Computer Science department for beginner coding classes. I enjoy helping students find and fix problems in their code while also learning so much for myself. My hobbies have always been on the creative side. I have enjoyed drawing, painting, composing music, singing, and performance throughout my life. There has always been a satisfaction that comes with creating something, which I have also experienced with software development.

Background
My professional background is actually in healthcare. I got my CNA license in 2019 while I was in high-school and have been working at a nursing home ever since. Working in this field has really strengthened my skills in communication, time management, and hard work. Although healthcare seems to be a rather unrelated field to technology, especially web development, there is a lot of room for advancement and improvement as facilities modernize their systems for medical charting, food services, and employee resources. Just from seeing the technology used at my facility, I think that healthcare-specialized software for food services would be extremely useful.

Skills
In order to be a successful team member, one needs to be able to connect effective communication with knowledge of their field. My background in healthcare helps me to be people and communication-focused, and my current experience and education in software engineering is teaching me technical skills. My experience with tutoring has been giving me a bridge between ‘people’ skills and technical skills. I think that my five greatest skills are professional communication, diligence, organization, creativity, and patience.

Why Hire Me?
I am a willing learner who strives to produce work that is organized as well as functional. I always want my code to be readable and efficient, and understand that learning goes far beyond the classroom. I consider myself a good team player as I am always concerned with meeting and eventually exceeding expectations of my teammates and supervisors. I think that my creativity and the satisfaction that comes with creation and visual beauty will also help me to stay motivated, especially on the front end of development.
