How Can I Help?

Greenwood rehabilitates and releases orphaned, sick or injured wildlife. This non-profit organization provides outreach programs that educates all age groups on rehabiliation, humane solutions to wildlife issues, and how to take action to advocate for Colorado's wildlife. Three strategies that Greenwood uses to help save Colorado wildlife is that it is volunteer and intern-run, it uses community outreach for funding, and it gathers donations in fun, heart-warming ways. You can participate in any of these areas by clicking the links below!

Volunteer or Intern

Since this organization is non-profit, Greenwood must be volunteer-run in order to afford other expenses. However, there is no short supply of volunteers looking to help care for animals. If you love animals and are willing to make this hands-on commitment, click to access volunteer applications. You can also apply for an internship internship at Greenwood to gain real-world experience in this area.

Outreach Programs

You can request a Greenwood representative to come to your school/church/organization, or use Greenwood's free virtual curriculum to educate yourself and others about rehabiliation and wildlife. If you would like to support children's education on the important issues of wildlife, you can also donate directly to Greenwood's free education content at the link as well.

Donate

There are many ways to donate to Greenwood Wildlife Center. You can donate generally to the organization, or sponser a particular kind of animal. You can also donate to Greenwood's Thrift Shop in Boulder, which brings in money to help these critters. Contributions help this organization to save thousands of wild animals in Colorado.
